Offerman and the University of Illinois

Nick Offerman began his life in a small town by the name of Minooka, Illinois near Joliet. In 1988, he came to the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign where he was enrolled in the Department of Theatre. During his freshman year, he lived in Scott Hall, room 380, where there is now a plaque hanging above the room number.

While in the Champaign area, Offerman was a part of many performances at the Krannert Center for Performing Arts such as the role of Alan in Picnic by William Inge. Offerman also performed in the plays Twelfth Night, A Christmas Carol, A Little Hotel on the Side, Biloxi Blues, Three Sisters, and Coriolanus. Along with performances organized by the University, he also organized plays in the free local theater spaces with classmates.
